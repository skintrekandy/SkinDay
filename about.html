About SkinDay

The cosmetic market is maturing.
Patients deserve better tools.

We built SkinDay because the way patients choose cosmetic clinics is broken, and the data proves it. Here's what we found, and what we're doing about it.

The Problem

When every clinic looks perfect,
nothing looks different.

Walk into any cosmetic clinic in Canada today and you'll find the same thing: a friendly smile, a 4.9-star Google rating, and a polished Instagram feed. The hard part isn't finding a clinic. It's knowing which one is actually right for you.

This isn't an accident. It's the result of a market that matured faster than its tools. As the number of clinics has exploded, over 3,800 across Canada and counting, the signals patients use to evaluate them have become nearly meaningless.

A 4.9-star rating means almost nothing when 70% of clinics share it. Patients are choosing based on proximity, aesthetics, and gut feel, not because those are good signals, but because better ones don't exist.

Our Thesis

Every mature market segments.
Cosmetics is next.

Look at any market that started as a commodity and you'll see the same arc: early chaos, consolidation, then segmentation. Coffee went from a commodity to specialty roasters to single-origin pour-overs. Hotels went from rooms to boutique experiences to lifestyle brands. The customers didn't change. Their sophistication did.

The cosmetic aesthetics market is following the same curve. Patients aren't going to stop getting Botox. They're going to get better at choosing who does it.

As the market grows more crowded, the clinics that win won't be the ones with the highest ratings. They'll be the ones that connect with the right patients, the patients who value their specific approach, their injector's credentials, their price point, their brand of care.

That connection doesn't happen by accident. It requires infrastructure: a way for patients to filter, compare, and choose with real context rather than guesswork.

Our Vision

The right patient for
the right clinic.

A patient who values a physician-led clinic shouldn't end up at a solo RN practice because the ratings looked the same. A budget-conscious first-timer shouldn't walk into a luxury boutique clinic expecting $8/unit pricing. A patient who wants an injector with 15 years of experience shouldn't be choosing blind.

These mismatches aren't just bad for patients, they're bad for clinics. A poor fit leads to unrealistic expectations, lower satisfaction, and reviews that don't reflect the clinic's actual strengths.

When patients find the clinic that's genuinely right for them, everyone wins, the patient gets better outcomes, the clinic gets a loyal client, and the market gets healthier.

SkinDay is building the infrastructure for that match to happen at scale, across every province, every price point, every credential type. Not a race to the bottom. A path to the right fit.
