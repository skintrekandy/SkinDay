Consultation photo guide

Good input photos are the single biggest factor in simulation quality. Each photo is checked automatically when you add it.

Lighting

  • Even, diffuse light from the front. Avoid strong side light, windows behind the patient, and overhead-only light.
  • No flash if possible; flash flattens the facial shading the simulation builds on.

Framing

  • Face fills roughly two-thirds of the frame, whole head and top of the neck visible.
  • Hair fully off the face (headband or clip), glasses off, neutral expression, mouth closed.
  • Camera at eye level, phone steady.

The three angles

  • Frontal: nose pointing straight at the camera, both ears equally visible.
  • Right 45°: patient turns so their right cheek faces the camera, about halfway to profile.
  • Left 45°: the same turn to the other side, left cheek toward the camera.

The 45° check: the dotted line through the nose tip should just touch the line of the far cheek. Nose well inside the cheek line means the turn is too shallow; nose clearly past it means too far toward profile.

Consistency

Use the same angles, distance, and lighting at every visit so before and after photos stay comparable over time.
