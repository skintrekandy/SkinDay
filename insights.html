Trends & Risks

Emerging market signals

Four structural shifts are reshaping the Canadian aesthetic market — and each one changes what patients should pay attention to when choosing a clinic.

Price Compression
32% of clinics are pricing below $7/unit
The budget segment is growing, particularly in suburban Ontario and newer markets. For patients, very low pricing can reflect a genuine high-volume, accessibility-first model — or it can mean fewer units and weaker results. The price alone doesn't tell you which.
Rating Inflation
63% of clinics are rated 4.9–5.0 on Google
Selective review solicitation has compressed ratings into an artificially narrow band. When nearly every clinic sits at 4.9–5.0, the star rating no longer helps patients tell clinics apart — which is exactly why pricing and credential transparency matter.
Market Expansion
Rapid clinic density growth outside the GTA
Secondary markets — Halifax, Winnipeg, Saskatoon — are emerging as meaningful clinic populations. Patients outside the major metros increasingly have real local options rather than having to travel to Toronto or Vancouver.
Channel Shift
Non-physician injectors now dominate by volume
RN and NP-led clinics make up most of the credentialed market. For patients, this means the registered nurse injecting you is the norm, not the exception — and asking about their specific injectable experience matters more than whether the clinic is "physician-led."
